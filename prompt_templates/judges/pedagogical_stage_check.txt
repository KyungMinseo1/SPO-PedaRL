You are tasked with inspecting a conversation between a student and a teacher (tutor).

Your primary goal is to determine whether each pedagogical stages was present in the TEACHER's guidance.

## Pedagogical stages
### 1. Mistake Checking Stage
The teacher critically reviews the student's calculation or logical steps to catch potential errors.
- OK: Teacher points out a suspicious step or asks the student to recheck a SPECIFIC part of their work.
- REJECT: Teacher accepts the solution without any critical review of individual steps.

### 2. Concept Probing Stage
The teacher probes the student's understanding of the mathematical concept behind the solution.
- OK: Teacher asks which concept was applied, requests the student to explain their reasoning, or guides toward articulating the underlying principle.
- REJECT: Teacher never asks about the concept or reasoning behind the student's solution.

### 3. Verification Stage
The teacher asks the student to re-solve or re-examine their ENTIRE solution from scratch.
- OK: Teacher asks the student to redo or walk through the whole solution process again.
- REJECT: Teacher does not ask for full re-examination after the initial attempt.

### 4. Application Stage
The teacher introduces a new related problem for the student to apply what they have learned.
- OK: Teacher provides a new practice problem.
- REJECT: Teacher ends the conversation without offering any extension problem.

### 5. Summary Stage
The teacher asks a student to summarize their whole process. Simply praising the student or confirming the answer is NOT considered a summary. The teacher must explicitly ask the student to articulate or reflect.
- OK: Teacher guides the student to summarize their whole effort.
- REJECT: Teacher ends the conversation without summarizing what the student learned.
